Bio

Dr. Brian S. Fugate is Associate Dean of Graduate Programs and Research in the Sam M. Walton College of Business at the University of Arkansas. He also serves as Chair of the J.B. Hunt Department of Supply Chain Management and holds the Oren Harris Endowed Chair in Transportation. Dr. Fugate previously served as a Fulbright Senior Research Scholar at the MIT-Zaragoza Logistics Center in Spain and as Co-Editor-in-Chief of the Journal of Supply Chain Management, the premier academic journal in the field. He has been recognized internationally for advancing research, graduate education, and executive development in logistics and supply chain management.

Dr. Fugate’s research program focuses on the strategic and global integration of demand and supply, knowledge flows, and sustainability across supply networks. His interdisciplinary work emphasizes collaboration, enabling technologies, and the social and environmental dimensions of supply chains. He has published in top journals including the Journal of Supply Chain Management, Journal of Operations Management, Decision Sciences, and the Academy of Management Journal. He is co-author of The Blockchain Toolkit: A Supply Chain Manager’s Guide to Understanding and Implementing Blockchain and the textbook Operations and Supply Chain Management: Enhancing Competitiveness and Customer Value. His scholarship has earned multiple awards, including the LaLonde Best Paper Award (2021, 2023) and the CSCMP E. Grosvenor Plowman Award (2017). He was also recognized among the Top 25 for Supply Chain Research Leadership (2001–2015).

Dr. Fugate has been repeatedly recognized as an award-winning educator. His teaching innovations have been honored with the AACSB’s “Innovations That Inspire” Award and multiple nominations for the Panhellenic Council’s University Professor of the Year. He teaches across undergraduate, graduate, executive, and professional programs, using both in-person and online formats, with a focus on experiential learning, simulations, industry projects, and active engagement. He has developed software tools and teaching cases that extend active learning globally. He frequently delivers executive education programs and professional development seminars for industry partners.

Prior to academia, Dr. Fugate gained extensive industry experience in worldwide logistics, supplier development, and industrial engineering. He worked with major organizations including Delta Air Lines, John Deere, Honeywell, and leading automotive suppliers. In these roles he focused on global logistics coordination, supplier performance improvement, industrial process design, and operations strategy. He continues to consult widely in areas such as sales and operations planning, lean management, demand forecasting, logistics optimization, and sustainability.

Under his leadership, the University of Arkansas supply chain program has achieved national recognition. The department was ranked #1 in North America by Gartner in 2020 for undergraduate supply chain education, only nine years after its launch. Dr. Fugate has been instrumental in forging industry partnerships, building executive MBA and specialty master’s programs, and ensuring the department’s curriculum prepares students with the analytical, technological, and interpersonal skills demanded by industry. His vision emphasizes continuous improvement, inclusion, and collaboration between academia and practice.
